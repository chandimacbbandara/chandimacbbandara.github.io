AI Car Damage Detection & Verification System

Computer vision pipeline for automated vehicle inspection using ResNet-18, ORB homography, and YOLOv8s.

A computer vision system that automates vehicle damage inspection by comparing before and after rental or insurance claim photos. It validates that both photos show the same vehicle, geometrically aligns them to account for camera angle differences, and then isolates only newly acquired damages — filtering out anything that was pre-existing. The full pipeline runs through a live Gradio dashboard deployed on Hugging Face Spaces.

  • ResNet-18 cosine similarity for vehicle identity verification.
  • ORB keypoint matching and RANSAC homography for perspective alignment.
  • Custom YOLOv8s model trained on 7 vehicle panel classes.
  • IoU-based filtering to isolate newly detected damage only.
  • Adjustable confidence threshold to eliminate false positives.

Overview

Traditional vehicle damage inspections are manual, subjective, and prone to disputes or fraud. This system replaces the manual process with a fully automated three-stage computer vision pipeline. It compares a vehicle's state before and after a rental period or insurance claim, verifying identity, correcting for camera angle differences, and precisely isolating only newly acquired damage across the four standard inspection views: Front, Rear, Left, and Right.

System Architecture

The pipeline is composed of three sequential vision stages:

  • Vehicle Identity Verification (ResNet-18): Extracts deep visual feature embeddings from both images. Calculates cosine similarity to confirm both photos represent the same vehicle from equivalent angles, rejecting mismatches before any damage analysis begins.
  • Perspective Alignment (ORB + Homography): Detects keypoints using ORB on both grayscale images, matches descriptors, and computes a homography matrix via RANSAC to warp the "After" image into alignment with the "Before" reference. This eliminates false positives caused by minor camera shifts.
  • Damage Detection & Filtering (YOLOv8s + IoU): Processes both aligned images through a custom-trained YOLOv8s model. Uses Intersection-over-Union filtering to identify bounding boxes that appear in the "After" state but were absent in "Before" — these are marked as new damage.

Dataset

The YOLOv8s model was trained on version 4 of the Capstone Vehicle Damage Dataset from Roboflow. The dataset contains annotated vehicle panels and damage across 7 classes: Bonnet, Bumper, Dickey, Door, Fender, Light, and Windshield. Null images (empty environment, no vehicles) were added to teach the model to ignore background false positives.

Model Training

The YOLOv8s model was trained for 50 epochs on a Tesla T4 GPU. The loss curves below show consistent convergence across box loss, classification loss, and DFL on both training and validation splits.

Challenges & Mitigations

  • Background False Positives: The model initially misidentified environmental structures (such as wooden carport beams) as damaged vehicle parts. This was mitigated by introducing null images — photos of the empty testing environment with no vehicles or bounding boxes — into the training set.
  • Inference Filtering: During live prediction, a configurable confidence threshold (adjustable via the Gradio UI) strictly filters out low-confidence detections. This ensures that bounding boxes are only drawn on genuine, high-confidence damage detections in production.
